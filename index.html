I — The Market

Sports is being financialised — into tradable markets.

Sports outcomes are becoming tradable financial contracts: bids, offers, spreads, market makers and continuous price discovery. City Road is not placing bets; we trade price, liquidity and risk in emerging markets where volume is scaling faster than institutional efficiency.

Traditional sportsbooks

Retail sportsbook model: house-against-bettor

  • Retail books price and manage risk directly
  • Edge is embedded as vig / overround in the quoted odds
  • Winning or sharp customers can be limited or refused
  • No open CLOB or continuous secondary market for bettors
Sports prediction markets

CLOB model: trader-against-trader

  • Transparent bid / ask spreads replace embedded sportsbook vig
  • Traders can cross the spread or post liquidity and earn it
  • No structural need to limit winning traders; risk sits with counterparties
  • Immature market-making and pricing infrastructure

IV — Why It's Still Inefficient

Liquid, but not yet institutionally efficient.

Six structural features keep prediction-market pricing loose enough to trade against — especially for disciplined, maker-led spread execution.

01

Fragmented liquidity

Depth is split across venues with no consolidated cross-market book.

02

Consumer-facing flow

Retail participation remains large, while professional flow is concentrated in selected markets.

03

Long-tail spread premia

Beyond the most liquid contracts, depth thins and executable spreads remain attractive.

04

Cross-venue price lag

Economically similar outcomes reprice at different speeds across books.

05

Uneven MM coverage

Professional liquidity is improving, but coverage remains inconsistent across tail, live and complex markets.

06

Sports / prop-market complexity

Long-tail leagues, props, live markets and combinatorial outcomes create persistent normalization errors.

V — Why Now

A three-driver window.

Prediction markets are crossing from retail-led venues to exchange-backed financial infrastructure — but pricing and execution infrastructure still lags volume growth.

01 · Venue growth

Volumes are scaling

Sports prediction-market volume rose from under $5B/mo in mid-2025 to ~$24B/mo combined by April 2026.

02 · Institutionalisation

Venues are professionalising

Custody, settlement and oversight are improving across both regulated and on-chain venues.

03 · Execution gap

Pricing infrastructure lags

Liquidity is growing faster than professional cross-venue pricing, basis normalisation, maker execution and risk systems.

Regulatory treatment remains evolving, but recent CFTC rulemaking activity may move the category toward clearer federal market structure.
