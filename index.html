IV — Why It's Still Inefficient

Liquid, but not yet efficient.

Six structural features keep pricing loose enough to trade against — and create room for disciplined, infrastructure-led execution.

01

Fragmented liquidity

Depth is split across venues with no consolidated book.

02

Consumer-led participation

Many venues are retail-facing; sharp flow is concentrated in narrow channels.

03

Wide spreads in long-tail markets

Beyond the most liquid contracts, spreads remain economically attractive.

04

Price-lag between venues

The same outcome reprices at different speeds across books.

05

Incomplete MM coverage

Few professional market makers operate continuously across these venues.

06

Domain-specific mispricing

Sports, event and tokenised assets lack mature reference pricing.

V — Why Now

A three-driver window.

Three forces are converging — and the efficiency gap is widest precisely while volumes are scaling fastest.

01 · Venue growth

Volumes are scaling

Crypto-native and regulated event venues are growing faster than legacy adjacencies.

02 · Institutionalisation

Venues are professionalising

03 · Efficiency gap

Pricing infrastructure lags

Professional pricing, market making and risk systems have not yet caught up with venue growth.

Regulatory treatment of event and on-chain markets remains evolving and is treated as a core risk-management workstream.

VI — Flow Composition

Consumer flow creates the initial inefficiency.

Many prediction and sports / event venues are consumer-facing. Direct retail-flow share is not consistently disclosed — so City Road treats it as a measurable research question, not an assumption.

Proxy 01

Trade-size distribution

Notional histograms separate small consumer tickets from institutional clips.

Proxy 02

Wallet clustering

On-chain venues allow address-level clustering and behavioural classification.

Proxy 03

Order-book behaviour

Cancel ratios, depth replenishment and quote dynamics reveal who is providing liquidity.

Proxy 04

Price impact

Markouts and impact decay separate informed from uninformed flow.

Sharp-vs-public price divergence is tracked across venues as an additional measure of informed-flow concentration.
